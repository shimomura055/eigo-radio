Instagram AI Privacy Backlash

Meta launched a new AI image feature called Muse Image during the week of July 7, 2026. One part of the feature let users use photos from public Instagram accounts as references when they generated AI images.

That option quickly caused criticism. Users and SAG-AFTRA, the performers' union, raised concerns about privacy and consent. They also worried that people could use the tool to create unauthorized digital likenesses, meaning AI-made images that resemble a person without that person's permission.

On July 10, Meta announced that the public-Instagram-reference part was no longer available. Meta did not remove all of Muse Image. The company removed only the function that used photos from public Instagram accounts as AI-image references.

Photos from public accounts were not simply a subject of conversation around the tool. Under this part of Muse Image, they could be used as references during the process of generating an AI image.

The change was specific to public-account references. It did not mean that every part of Muse Image had disappeared. The remaining system and the removed reference function are two different parts of this story.

Today's Muse Image Privacy Points

Point One

Why Critics Objected

Users and SAG-AFTRA, the performers' union, criticized the feature over privacy and consent. Their concerns were linked to the use of public Instagram photos as AI-image references.

Another concern was unauthorized digital likenesses. A digital likeness is an image that looks like a real person. Critics worried that AI images could resemble people without their permission.

Privacy, consent, and digital likenesses are related but different concerns. Privacy and consent concern the use of photos, while digital likenesses concern AI images that may look like people.

Point Two

What Changed, What Stayed

Meta's July 10 announcement was limited. The public-Instagram-reference function was no longer available, but the Muse Image system itself remained.

This distinction matters for understanding the news. Meta did not announce an end to AI image generation through Muse Image. It announced the removal of one specific way to provide references: photos from public Instagram accounts.

The timing is also important. Meta launched Muse Image during the week of July 7, then announced the change on July 10. The announcement came after criticism.

In One Line

After criticism over privacy, consent, and digital likenesses, Meta removed Muse Image's public Instagram photo reference function days after launching it.

Muse Image itself was not removed.

The story centers on a specific AI-image reference option, not the entire system.
